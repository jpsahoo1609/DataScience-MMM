# Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) - Linear Regression using PySpark

## Project Overview

This project demonstrates a simple Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) approach using PySpark Linear Regression to quantify the impact of advertising spend on sales.

The objective is to identify which marketing channel contributes the most to sales and estimate the incremental return generated by each advertising channel.

---

## Business Problem

A company invests in multiple advertising channels:

- TV Advertising
- Radio Advertising
- Newspaper Advertising

The business wants to understand:

- Which advertising channel drives the highest sales impact?
- How strongly is each channel associated with sales?
- Where should future marketing budgets be allocated?

---

## Dataset

Features:

- TV Ad Budget ($)
- Radio Ad Budget ($)
- Newspaper Ad Budget ($)

Target Variable:

- Sales ($)

---

## Solution Approach

### 1. Data Preparation

- Loaded data into PySpark DataFrame
- Performed exploratory analysis
- Checked correlations between marketing channels and sales

### 2. Feature Engineering

Used `VectorAssembler` to combine advertising spend columns into a single feature vector.

```python
features = [
    "TV Ad Budget ($)",
    "Radio Ad Budget ($)",
    "Newspaper Ad Budget ($)"
]
```

### 3. Train-Test Split

```python
train_df, test_df = data.randomSplit([0.8, 0.2], seed=42)
```

- 80% Training Data
- 20% Testing Data

### 4. Model Training

Applied PySpark Linear Regression:

```python
lr = LinearRegression(
    featuresCol="features",
    labelCol="Sales ($)"
)

model = lr.fit(train_df)
```

### 5. Prediction

Generated predictions on unseen test data:

```python
predictions = model.transform(test_df)
```

---

## Model Results

### Regression Coefficients

| Channel | Coefficient |
|----------|------------:|
| TV | 0.0468 |
| Radio | 0.1856 |
| Newspaper | 0.0003 |

---

## Business Interpretation

### Radio Advertising

- Highest impact on sales
- Strongest ROI among all channels
- Every additional \$1 spent on Radio advertising is associated with approximately \$0.1856 increase in sales

### TV Advertising

- Positive contribution to sales
- Lower impact than Radio
- Every additional \$1 spent on TV advertising is associated with approximately \$0.0468 increase in sales

### Newspaper Advertising

- Negligible sales impact
- Minimal incremental return observed

---

## Channel Ranking

1. Radio Advertising ✅
2. TV Advertising ✅
3. Newspaper Advertising ⚠️

---

## Key Findings

- Radio is the most effective channel for driving sales.
- TV contributes positively but is significantly less effective than Radio.
- Newspaper advertising shows almost no measurable impact on sales.
- Marketing budget optimization should prioritize Radio spend.
