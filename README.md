# DataScience-MMM

A collection of Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) and statistical modeling projects focused on measuring the impact of marketing activities on business outcomes.

This repository demonstrates foundational and advanced techniques commonly used in Marketing Analytics, Marketing Science, and Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM), including:

- Linear Regression
- Mixed Effects Models
- Bayesian Modeling

The projects focus on understanding how marketing investments contribute to sales while accounting for uncertainty, business hierarchy, and real-world data complexity.

# Learning Path

The projects are intentionally structured to demonstrate progression in statistical modeling:

```text
Linear Regression
        ↓
Mixed Effects Modeling
        ↓
Bayesian Modeling
```

### Evolution of Complexity

#### Linear Regression

Assumes all observations behave similarly.

#### Mixed Effects Model

Accounts for group-level differences such as stores, regions, or markets.

#### Bayesian Model

Captures uncertainty and provides probabilistic estimates for decision-making.

# Business Context

Marketing Mix Modeling is widely used to answer questions such as:

- Which marketing channels drive sales?
- What is the return on advertising spend?
- How effective are promotional campaigns?
- How should marketing budgets be allocated?
- How can uncertainty be incorporated into decision-making?

The methodologies implemented in this repository address these questions at increasing levels of statistical sophistication.
